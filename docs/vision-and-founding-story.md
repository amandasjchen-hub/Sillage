# Sillage — Vision & Founding Story

**Written:** 2026-05-30  
**Author:** Amanda Chen (founder)

---

## The Vision

Sillage is the hub app for perfume lovers — a place to discover, archive, and share your fragrance world.

The closest reference points are **Fragrantica** (the existing fragrance database) and **Co-Star** (the astrology app). Sillage is the combination: Fragrantica's depth and completeness, but with Co-Star's beautiful design, playful personality, and social energy.

The goal is not just a utility app. It's a world that perfume lovers want to live in.

---

## Why Perfume

**Amanda Chen** is a perfume content creator with 400+ bottles in her personal collection and 50,000 followers who engage deeply with fragrance content. She built Sillage first for herself — to archive her collection, log her wears, and discover what to buy next.

Fragrance is having a major cultural moment. It's booming. But the infrastructure hasn't caught up:

- People are spending serious money on perfume (bottles regularly cost $200–$500+)
- There are rich stories behind every fragrance — notes, houses, perfumers, origins
- The community is passionate and growing fast
- But the tools available are outdated, ugly, or too niche

**Fragrantica** is the dominant player, but it's a cautionary tale: a mishmash of data accumulated over decades, with design that hasn't evolved, and a UI so dense it intimidates newcomers. They're too far in to change. This is the gap.

Someone can build something fun, personalized, and curated — and capture this moment before it passes.

## The Market Gap

| What exists | What's missing |
|-------------|---------------|
| Fragrantica — comprehensive but ugly and overwhelming | Beautiful, modern design that feels like a luxury product |
| Reddit fragrance communities — passionate but unstructured | A structured social layer with profiles, feeds, and discovery |

| Nothing | Playful, personality-driven features (astrology by perfume, mood matching, etc.) |

## Who Sillage Is For

Three distinct but overlapping users:

### 1. The Collector
Serious perfume lovers with large collections (10, 50, 400+ bottles). They want to:
- Archive everything they own
- Log when they wear something and how it made them feel
- Track their wishlist and blind buy risks
- See their collection analyzed and organized in beautiful ways

### 2. The Curious
People who are interested in fragrance but don't know where to start. They want to:
- Discover new fragrances based on what they already like
- Understand notes, families, and houses without feeling overwhelmed
- Get recommendations they can trust

### 3. The Social User
People drawn in by the fun, personality-driven layer. They want to:
- See what friends are wearing
- Engage with content like "your perfume astrology" or "your scent personality"
- Share their taste and discover theirs through a lens that feels fresh and playful

## Why Now

- Fragrance is a booming category — sales and cultural interest are at an all-time high
- Gen Z and Millennials are investing in fragrance as personal expression
- Social media has created a generation of perfume creators and enthusiasts
- The incumbent (Fragrantica) is architecturally stuck and can't redesign
- AI makes it possible to build rich, personalized discovery experiences that weren't feasible before

---

## Design Philosophy

Sillage should feel like a **luxury product that's also warm and human**. Not cold and scientific like a database. Not chaotic like a forum. Something that makes you feel like your taste is seen and celebrated.

References:
- **Co-Star** — personality, wit, beautiful typography, makes data feel personal
- **Fragrantica** — comprehensiveness, community trust, depth of information (but not the design)
- **Are.na / Notion** — calm, organized, gives space for thought
- **Depop / VSCO** — social energy, creator-first
